# ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Clause Summary

**Standard:** ISO/IEC 27001:2022  
**Purpose:** Information Security Management Systems (ISMS)  
**Prepared by:** Sruti Susan Shaji | ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Certified Lead Auditor

---

## Overview

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 is the international standard for establishing, 
implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Information 
Security Management System (ISMS). It follows the Plan-Do-Check-Act 
(PDCA) cycle across Clauses 4–10.

---

## Clauses 1–3 — Foundational

### Clause 1 — Scope
Defines the boundaries and applicability of the ISO 27001 standard. 
Organisations must clearly define what is within the scope of their ISMS.

### Clause 2 — Normative References
References ISO/IEC 27000, which provides the vocabulary and definitions 
used throughout the standard.

### Clause 3 — Terms & Definitions
Establishes common language used across the standard — including key 
terms such as asset, risk, control, and information security.

---

## Clauses 4–10 — ISMS Requirements (PDCA Cycle)

### Clause 4 — Context of the Organisation *(Plan)*
The organisation must understand its internal and external context, 
identify interested parties and their requirements, and define the 
scope of the ISMS accordingly.

### Clause 5 — Leadership *(Plan)*
Top management must demonstrate commitment to information security. 
This includes establishing an information security policy, assigning 
roles and responsibilities, and ensuring accountability at the 
leadership level.

### Clause 6 — Planning *(Plan)*
Requires the organisation to identify information security risks, 
assess and treat them, maintain a risk register, and set measurable 
information security objectives.

### Clause 7 — Support *(Do)*
Covers the resources, competence, awareness, communication, and 
documented information needed to support the ISMS. Ensures the right 
people, tools, and documentation are in place.

### Clause 8 — Operation *(Do)*
Focuses on implementing and controlling the processes planned in 
Clause 6. This is where the ISMS moves from planning into actual 
day-to-day execution.

### Clause 9 — Performance Evaluation *(Check)*
The organisation must monitor, measure, analyse, and evaluate the 
performance of the ISMS through internal audits and management reviews 
to ensure it is working effectively.

### Clause 10 — Improvement *(Act)*
Addresses nonconformities, corrective actions, and continual 
improvement. Organisations must respond to failures and ensure the 
ISMS keeps evolving and improving over time.

---

## PDCA Summary

| Phase | Clauses | Focus |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Plan | 4, 5, 6 | Understand, Lead, Plan |
| Do | 7, 8 | Support, Operate |
| Check | 9 | Monitor, Audit, Review |
| Act | 10 | Improve, Correct |

---

## Key Takeaway

ISO 27001 is not just a technical standard — it is a management 
framework that requires commitment from leadership, clear processes, 
documented evidence, and a culture of continual improvement.
